Strategic Management Programme (SMP)

Empowering leaders to adapt and excel in a rapidly changing world.

Programme Details

Overview

SMP is ideal for experienced strategists who wish to be refreshed and updated on the most modern approaches to Strategic Management. The ability of organizations and individuals to adapt and change is now the norm – not the exception.

The course takes on the perpetual change from three perspectives: firstly, by introducing a new strategy; secondly, by adapting the strategy to changing circumstances; and thirdly, by reacting to abrupt and unexpected circumstances. We will analyse how to “think the unthinkable” and anticipate risks and crises, so that they can be professionally managed, when and if they arise.

It wraps with a summary of the key learning points, followed by an action planning exercise with a view to apply the acquired knowledge and skills immediately upon participant’s return to work. Post-programme support is available in relation to the implementation of a participant’s action plan, up to six (6) months following programme completion.

Programme Content

  • Strategic Analysis
  • Ten-step strategic planning process
  • Current organisational structure, characteristics, and context
  • Internal and external analysis
  • Developing the Strategic Plan
  • Agreeing, understanding, and communicating the vision
  • Evaluating alternative strategic approaches
  • Stakeholder Identification, Analysis, and Engagement
  • Strategic Leadership and Management

Target Participants

This course is suitable for:

  • C-Suite Officers
  • Board Chairs and Members
  • Company Secretaries
  • Non-executive Directors
  • Directors, Deputy Directors, and Senior Managers
  • Heads of Departments and Supervisors

Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, participants will have an excellent grasp of how to implement a successful strategy, including:

  • Tools to analyse where an organization is currently positioned
  • Importance of creating a vision and developing a robust plan
  • Harnessing stakeholder interest and influence
  • Allocating resources to maximize capacity and capability
  • Leadership committed to Strategic Management Excellence
  • Incorporating change into strategic thinking
  • Measuring and monitoring progress and results
  • Planning for risks and crises – “thinking the unthinkable”
  • Implementing an Action Plan at the workplace
